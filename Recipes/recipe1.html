Fried Rice

This is a Fried Rice recipe I have used for the past five years as a base. Fried Rice is a form of base food, where ingriedients can change based on whats sitting in your fridge. You do not need a wok, but a pan that can very evenly distributed heat like cast iron will work very well. It also pays to chop things ahead of time, so one can add ingridients at a good time to ensure crispiness. I reccommend brunoice cut(dicing into cubes, doesnt have to be precise) for most of the vegatables, outside of the ones specified otherwise.

Ingriedients

  • Day Old white rice or brown rice, cool not warm.-1 cup
  • Soy Sauce-2 oz or 4 to 5 tablespoons
  • One Carrot
  • Half of one onion or a bunch of spring onions
  • One celery stalk
  • 2 table spoons brown sugar or honey
  • One egg, scrambled
  • two garlic cloves minced
  • 3 snack peppers or one whole bell pepper
  • toasted or regular sesame oil- 2 tablespoons
  • ginger, grated around 2 tablespoons or minced
  • one teaspoon black pepper
  1. Begin by heating up a pan to medium heat, adding one tablespoon of your oil, adding your egg and keep it moving to turn it scrambled. Cook until golden yellow pieces form, or three to four mins.
  2. Take your chopped carrot, celery stalk, snack peppers and spring onions with the leftover oil and fry for for two to three minutes, until vegetables are browned
  3. Take the soy sauce, ginger, garlic, and brown sugar/honey and whisk together for 30 seconds.
  4. Add rice to your pan and stir it together with the vegetables.Allow one or two minutes of frying to get rice crispy and such.
  5. Add sauce and stir to spread it evenly. Cook for 2 to 3 mins until fully incorporated and liquid is evaporated.
  6. Serve and Enjoy! you can also take some of the spring onion and mince on side for a garnish! Much love and Kindness
