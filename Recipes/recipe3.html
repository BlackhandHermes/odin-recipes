Mushroom Soup

This is a mushroom soup I have made for my family several times. I used to call it scrap soup because I used to clear out the fridge of older veggies and wilty mushrooms. After some time, we upgraded it to have a better flavor and more consistent ingriedients. One can even add cream if one desires a different taste profile. Soup is something you want to create in layers, to maximize flavors and textures. Do it slow, and watch as your soup turns to deliciousness!

Ingredients

  • Shitake Mushrooms, 12 ounces
  • Sweet or White onion, Large,One
  • One large carrot or several small ones.
  • One Stalk of celery
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • Shitake Mushrooms, 12 ounces(remove stems and keep them, very well minced, the caps slice julienne )
  • Sweet or White onion, Large,One,diced
  • One large carrot or several small ones,diced
  • One Stalk of celery, minced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • Broth, Chicken, 4 quarts worth, or bullion two cubes dissolved in water
  • 2 giant spoonfuls of honey
  • Parsley, one bunch
  • Thyme, one bunch
  • Black pepper, One tablespoon
  • Garlic powder,Two table spoons
  • Onion powder,Two tablespoons
  • White wine or beer, for deglazing, a splash lol

Steps

  1. Begin by bringing a pot to heat with some oil and all your vegetables(no herbs), and sautee them for 5 mins. Use a splash of wine or beer to deglaze
  2. Add your broth, and bring to a boil once and let it simmer at medium low heat for the next 20 mins
  3. Add all you seasonings and herbs, and let simmer to incorporate 5 mins
  4. Add the honey to thicken and balance the flavors out.
  5. Salt to taste, and enjoy! You can choose to retain some of your parsley as a garnish.
